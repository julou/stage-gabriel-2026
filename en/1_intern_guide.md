## What you will be doing

You will be working in a research laboratory that studies how bacteria
grow, divide, and respond to their environment.

Your internship is organised around **two big scientific questions**:

### 🔬 Question 1 — How does a simple bacterium produce exponential growth?

A bacterium divides in two. Its two daughters divide in two. And so on.
This very simple rule, repeated thousands of times, produces the J-shaped growth curves
you will measure in the lab — and simulate yourself on a computer.

You will also see what happens when nutrients run out: growth stops.
And you will observe a classic experiment called *diauxie*: when given
two different sugars, bacteria sometimes consume them one after the other — not simultaneously.
This reveals that bacteria *regulate* their own genes depending on what is available.

### 💡 Question 2 — How does a bacterium control whether a gene is switched on?

The bacteria you will study have been genetically modified to produce
a green fluorescent protein (GFP). When the gene is active, the bacterium emits more light.

You will measure how a chemical signal (a molecule called "IPTG") activates this gene,
plot the response curve, and compare it to a simple mathematical model.
Two variants of the same gene respond differently — you will measure this difference.
